Errata

Where significant alterations have been made to the original 1907 edition, they are noted below:

General

&c.
In the original, “etc.” and “&c.” are used interchangeably; this edition uses “&c.” throughout.
Carcase
Whilst it is an acceptable variant spelling, it has been changed to the O.E.D.’s preferred “carcass”.
Coffee-spoonful
In the original, coffee-spoonful and coffeespoonful are used interchangeably, I have used coffee-spoonful throughout.
Coulis
Cullises changed to Coulis
Gray
Changed gray to grey.
Half glaze
In the original, half glaze and half-glaze are used interchangeably, I have used half glaze throughout.
Meat glaze
In the original, meat glaze and meat-glaze are used interchangeably, I have used meat glaze throughout.
Profiteroles
Changed the, now rather archaic, profiterolles to profiteroles.
Recipe references
In the original, both “Formula 102” and “Formula №102” are used to refer to recipes, this edition uses “Formula №102” throughout.
Sauté-pan
In the original, sauté-pan and sautépan are used interchangeably, I have used sauté-pan throughout.
Pineapple
Pine-apple has been changed to pineapple throughout.

Specific

Formula №10 (White Veal Stock, Poultry Stock)

The original contains a misprint for the last listed ingredient:

Ingredient Amount
Quantity Unit
faggot, including 1 oz. of parsley, 1 bay leaf, and a of parsley, 1 bay leaf, and a 1

Based upon Escoffier Online’s translation from the Complete guide, I have changed it to:

Ingredient Amount
Quantity Unit
Faggot, including 1 oz. of parsley, 1 bayleaf, and a small sprig of thyme. 1
Consommé aux Profiteroles
Corrected formula reference from formula №28 to formula №228.
Consommé Richelieu
Existing list was inline for the first item and then a block list for the second two items. Changed all to inline.
